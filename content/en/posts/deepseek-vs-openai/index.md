I had my agent running — researching, summarizing, generating reports. It was beautiful. Then I checked my OpenAI billing dashboard. **$20 gone. Two hours.**

I hadn't set any limits. I hadn't even thought about token consumption. I was treating API calls like they were free, and OpenAI was happy to let me.

## Switching to DeepSeek V4

Someone pointed me to DeepSeek. I was skeptical — you get what you pay for, right?

Wrong.

DeepSeek V4 costs a **fraction** of what OpenAI charges per million tokens. For the kind of work my agent does — research, structured analysis, report generation — the quality is at par. Not "almost." At par.

I'm not saying DeepSeek beats GPT-4 on every benchmark. But for the workloads that actually matter in production — summarization, structured output, multi-step reasoning — the performance gap is negligible and the cost gap is enormous.

## Token Budgets Change Behavior

Here's something I didn't expect: when I gave myself a token budget, I became more thoughtful about every prompt.

Think about what that means for a team. If your developers and analysts have unlimited API access with no visibility into cost, they'll burn tokens without thinking. Give them a budget and suddenly they're writing tighter prompts, caching results, and questioning whether every call is necessary.

**Token budgets don't just save money. They create better engineers.**

## What I Spend Now

I won't share exact numbers yet (more data coming), but my monthly inference cost dropped by over 90% after switching. My agent runs more tasks, produces better output, and costs less than a Netflix subscription.

## The Takeaway

If you're building AI agents or automation, **model cost is a design decision**, not an afterthought. Switching to DeepSeek was the single highest-leverage change I made to my whole setup — more impact than any prompt optimization or architecture tweak.
